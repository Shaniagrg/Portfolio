AR App

The Product

The AR hotel application enhances the guest experience through immersive virtual tours, interactive maps, and personalized concierge services. The aim is to elevate guest satisfaction by seamlessly blending digital and physical experiences.

Project duration

Apr 2024 - Jul 2024

The problem

A survey by AHLA found that 67% of hotels report staffing shortages and guests needing assistance from hotel staff often face long waits and go without help due to staffing shortages, leading to frustration and unmet needs

The goal

My objective was to create an AR experience to support hotel industry, that would foster a sense of welcome and engagement to attract more customers through our app with minimal assistance from hotel staff

My role

UX designer designing app for Hotel that is engaging and enjoyable for users

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs and responsive design

Understanding User

In conducting user research, we tried to gain a deeper understanding of the challenges faced by hotel guests and their expectations regarding engagement with hotel services. Through surveys, interviews, and observational studies, we discovered that many customers often felt overlooked and uninformed about the availability of complimentary hotel amenities until after their stay had concluded. Sometimes customer also felt overlooked and uninformed about the availability of complimentary hotel amenities until after their stay had concluded. This lack of awareness led to a sense of disconnection and missed opportunities for engagement during their visit. Furthermore, guests expressed a desire for more interactive and immersive experiences that would enhance their overall stay. These insights guided our approach in designing an AR solution aimed at addressing these pain points and creating a more welcoming and engaging experience for hotel guests.

User research: pain points

Missed Amenities

Guests often discover complimentary amenities only after their stay, leading to missed opportunities and dissatisfaction

No interactive experience

Users desire more interactive experiences beyond traditional amenities to enhance their stay

Lack of Engagement

Many feel overlooked during their stay, resulting in a lack of engagement with hotel services

Confused using app

Some guests struggle to access relevant information about hotel amenities, causing confusion and uncertainty

User Journey Map

Developing a user journey map for Emily navigating the hotel helped me pinpoint potential pain points and areas for enhancement. By visualizing her entire experience, I was able to identify opportunities to improve the app's usability and overall guest satisfaction.

Paper Wireframes

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points. For home screen, I prioritized a quick and easy process to help users save time.

Digital Wireframes

As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Low-fidelity Prototype

The low fidelity prototype was designed to establish the core user flow of searching for hotel amenities. This prototype emphasized the basic navigation and interaction elements, enabling users to locate different amenities such as the gym, pool, and dining areas virtually. The primary aim was to validate the concept and ensure that the virtual map functionality was intuitive and straightforward. This initial version was instrumental in conducting usability studies, where real users provided feedback on the fundamental design and navigation, allowing for essential adjustments before the final version.

View Low-fidelity

Usability Study Finding

Round 1 findings

  • 70% of user wanted to start up with sign in page for real experience
  • 65% of user wanted to have a page with overview of the hotel amenities
  • 60% of user was confused having two button on same image

Round 2 findings

  • 80% of user wanted immersive view
  • 90% of user wanted to browse the map before showing direction through camera

Mockups

Highlighting the impact of usability testing on design changes, we added icons to the top right of the search bar to help users focus on what they want. Additionally, I revised the design to include headings for amenities with short descriptions that appear when hovering over them.

This section illustrates the before and after of our design process. Considering user needs, I included only one button instead of two, as many users were confused by having two buttons on one image.

High-fidelity Prototype

The high-fidelity prototype showcased a polished and refined user experience within the hotel app, integrating advanced AR features directly on the phone. This version focused on delivering seamless and efficient user flows, ensuring that navigating through hotel amenities was both visually appealing and functioning well. Enhanced graphical elements and interactions were implemented to create a more engaging experience. Additionally, the high-fidelity prototype provided an immersive view when used with AR glasses and easily through phone camera offering users an enriched, navigation experience. This final prototype aimed to closely mimic the intended end product, incorporating detailed visual and interactive elements to facilitate more accurate user testing and feedback.

Accessibility consideration

Integrated voice commands for hands-free navigation

Added clear visual directions and icons in the AR map to help users navigate the hotel without needing staff assistance

Provided an option for a simplified view to reduce screen clutter and make navigation easier

Takeaways

Impact

Users found the AR navigation feature immensely helpful, as it allowed them to easily find their way around the hotel without needing to ask staff. The clear visual directions and icons enhanced their overall experience, making exploration more enjoyable and efficient.

One user noted:

“The app looked more soothing to the eyes and made me browse before directly jumping on searching the hotel amenities. It also gave me an immersive view of the hotel amenities and directed me. I would definitely use this app on my every stay at a hotel.”

What I learned

This project taught me the value of integrating intuitive AR features to streamline user navigation and improve their experience. I learned how essential it is to provide clear visual guidance and minimize the need for external assistance, making technology both practical and engaging for users.

Next Steps
